History

Simon Sounds is more than just a music store—it's a beloved cultural cornerstone with roots stretching back to the 19th century. Originally built as an agricultural hall in the 1880s, the building served as a hub for local farmers and tradespeople. In 1976, it was transformed into a music store called Simon TV and Music Shop, and in 1977, the name was changed to Simon Sounds to reflect its growing focus on music. A storefront renovation in 1985 gave it its now-iconic look, blending historic charm with vibrant creativity. Since 2019, Simon Sounds has been a workers' cooperative, emphasizing its commitment to community and collaboration.

Values

At Simon Sounds Workers Cooperative, we are dedicated to empowering artists and fostering a vibrant community through music in the heart of the Pioneer Valley. We provide high-quality instruments, expert repairs, personalized lessons, and welcoming rehearsal spaces—all designed to inspire creativity and collaboration. As a worker-owned business by musicians, we are rooted in cooperation and a shared love for the art of sound, striving to make music accessible, sustainable, and transformative for everyone who walks through our doors.

Community

We believe music thrives when it's shared. That's why we're dedicated to building a strong, supportive community for musicians of all levels in the Pioneer Valley and beyond.
